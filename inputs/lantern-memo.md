## Lantern: The Intelligence Layer for Blue-Collar Work

### Background
America is in the midst of a real-world renaissance. After decades of offshoring, the physical economy is being rebuilt: re-industrialization is pulling factories back onshore, the largest data-center buildout in history is underway, and aging infrastructure is coming due. All of these trends require skilled tradespeople who maintain and build our physical world. However, the blue-collar workforce is not growing to meet the moment. Five skilled tradespeople retire for every two who enter. McKinsey estimates that by 2032, the trades will need ~22x more hires than the number of net-new jobs created. We cannot only recruit our way out of a gap this shape. We must also up-skill our labor force.

### The Intelligence layer for blue-collar work
Over the last five years, LLMs have transformed and up-skilled white-collar workers. The pattern is repeating industry by industry: Cursor ($60b) for engineers, Harvey ($11b) and Legora ($5.6b) in legal, Abridge ($5.3b) for medical. That same transformation is needed in blue-collar work. The challenge: blue-collar workers won't adopt LLMs the way knowledge workers have. They don't sit at a keyboard and have no digital footprint; their work and hard-won expertise is ephemeral. Reaching them means meeting them where they work: hands-free copilots focused on audio and video, because what a tradesperson sees and says carries the information. Lantern captures what a skilled blue-collar worker sees and says, and agentically turns it into tangible work product. Starting with the trade that offers the fastest path to adoption: auto servicing.

### The first product: smart safety glasses for auto mechanics
AI safety glasses that instantly up-skill auto mechanics. Wedge entry = dealerships + vehicle inspection.
Current workflow: mechanic performs ~15-min multi-point inspection (MPI) → fills ~40-question MPI checklist → records ~90-sec video MPI → service advisor reviews → video+quote sent to customer.
Solution: smart safety glasses record the inspection hands-free, then automate away the MPI checklist, video MPI, advisor review, and quoting. Lantern creates AI-edited and voiced-over customer-ready video MPIs optimized for upsells, translatable into any language. The tech keeps working; the documentation and sales asset are produced for them.

### Problems we solve / why it matters
- **Video MPI creation:** Dealers using video MPIs see 20-30% increase in customer-pay labor dollars per RO. Granbury Nissan saw +$150/RO. J.D. Power: 51% of customers receiving photo/video MPI have recommended work done vs 24% without. But the process is tedious; low attachment rates; poor quality; language barriers; techs lack incentive/skill to make good sales videos. OEMs are beginning to REQUIRE video MPIs — top of mind for every service manager.
- **Time saving:** Techs lose ~1 hour/day to paperwork. Stop the job, remove gloves, phone with greasy hands, app, photos, type notes. Messy notes passed to service advisor to interpret/translate.
- **Poor customer experience:** No way to ask questions, learn about upsold parts, understand timing.

### How we solve it
End-to-end: hands-free audio/video recording of the inspection → beautiful UX optimized for upselling. Glasses ensure 100% attachment rate of educational videos → more upsells than traditional MPIs. Saves mechanics + advisors hours/week. As we scale, more video+customer data → models learn what phrasing/editing/UX drives highest revenue per RO → virtuous cycle.

### Why auto first
- No behavioral change: mechanics already wear safety glasses; Lantern replaces something on their face.
- Centralized & measurable: mechanics work in one shop (vs dispersed trades). Adoption easy to monitor/enforce.
- They already buy software: DMS (Cox, CDK, Reynolds) can cost $10,000/mo/dealer. One design partner (5 dealers) spends $3m/yr on software.
- Service is the profit center: maintenance shop = ~half of dealer gross profit.
- Fast to test: dealers move quickly trialing software.
- Social & insular: industry talks to itself; peer networks (20 Groups) spread working products by word of mouth.

### Hardware
Starting with off-the-shelf: testing Meta Ray-Ban, Oakley Vanguard, Insta360 3S clipped to hats. Beginning conversations with joint development manufacturers in China for a glasses order. Advantage over consumer glasses: don't need aesthetic tradeoffs; safety glasses are larger → can add battery + processing.
