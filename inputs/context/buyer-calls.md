## Call 1 — Dwayne Kinsley, Corporate Fixed-Operations Director, LA Car Guy (Sullivan Automotive Group) — Feb 26
- 12 dealerships across LA. Brands: Toyota, Lexus, Porsche, Audi, VW, Subaru, Honda, Kia, **Hyundai, Genesis**. All import/luxury. Service center every store, 10-50 bays.
- Oversees all service + parts (after-sales) for the group. Org: service manager → (assistant/drive mgr) → advisors; techs on **flat rate** (paid per job, CA hourly + production incentive).
- Stack: **CDK** (main DMS), MyKaarma (payments/comms), Call Review (call analytics), CRM/BDC; **recently moved away from XTime + DealerFX**, brought MPI/video back in-house with CDK. AI inbound: **Mia (4 stores), Pam (5 stores)** — picks Mia for lower latency.
- Tech admin time: ~1.5 hrs of 8-hr shift on data entry / photos / documentation.
- Korean brands (Hyundai/Genesis) require photo of every old part on every repair → heavy documentation.
- Warranty: won't get claim paid without documentation. **5-7% of monthly claims short-paid**. Warranty mix 30% industry-standard up to **60-70% (Genesis), ~40% (Porsche)**.
- Media attach: **~85% of MPI packets; ~60% of ASRs** (Additional Service Requests — where it matters most for conversion).
- Two video types: (a) MPI walkaround = transparency/trust, doesn't sell; (b) ASR "here's what's wrong" = the real upsell driver. "We sell what people can't see."
- Service advisor packet review: ~10 min/RO closeout, ~1-1.5 hr/day; **Lexus store has 9 advisors on duty**.
- Tried/looked at: Pencil Wrench, Rapid RO (tech story help), kiosks (virtual service advisor). DMS antiquated, "pay to play" integration, things don't sync.
- Mentioned an OEM (thinks Mercedes) doing tech-line / camera-glasses; AR glasses with preloaded work instructions.
GOLD QUOTES:
- "Sign me up!" (reaction to the glasses pitch)
- "I would love to get partnered up with a private equity firm that wants to spend some money and build a new platform because most people in the car business will tell you we wish we could just have one tool that would do everything... most people would pay."
- On legacy tools: "It's stale... old tech that someone once upon a time built. And then companies like Cox Automotive buys them up and never improves upon them. XTime used to be the coolest tool 10 years ago... it's the same as it always was."
- On AI vendors vs legacy: "when you ask them to tweak and change things, they're like, yeah, let's do it... 20-something-year-old coder in the background."
- "Automating customer packet review would allow me to eliminate Service Advisor headcount" (+ more customer-facing time).
- Additional idea (excited): remote OEM tech-line support with shared vision through the glasses.

## SYNTHESIZED SIGNAL TAXONOMY (how to map the universe of Lantern targets)
ICP = **multi-location franchised auto dealership groups, US (+ Canada)**. (18,000+ franchised US dealerships; 40%+ in multi-location groups; most 2-5 rooftops, many regional 10-50, largest hundreds.)

1. **Rooftop count + bays/lifts + tech/advisor headcount** → Opportunity / ROI-sizing axis.
2. **Brand mix → warranty & documentation intensity** (Korean Hyundai/Genesis 60-70% warranty + mandatory photo-of-every-part; luxury Porsche/Land Rover/BMW; domestic Ford/Stellantis/GM = brutal first-time claim denials). → **WOW axis**: brand mix predicts both documentation pain AND warranty-$ leakage Lantern recovers.
3. **Legacy MPI/video-tool footprint** (XTime/DealerFX/AutoPoint/MyKaarma/CDK-MPI/Status Plus/See It Now). Attach rate spread **20-95%** → low attach = open pain; XTime-incumbent = displacement OR integration target.
4. **DMS** (CDK/Reynolds ~70% share; Tekion = modern). Integration is table-stakes; non-integrating glasses = non-starter.
5. **AI-adoption posture** — already buying voice AI (Mia/Pam/near), OEM AR-glasses subscriptions → receptive, early-adopter signal.
6. **PE-backed / consolidating / fast-growing groups** = sophisticated, ROI-driven buyers deploying across many rooftops.

SEGMENTATION INSIGHT: sophisticated high-attach operators (McGovern 94%, LA Car Guy 85%) see glasses as *incremental*, pilot only w/ integration + repair libraries. Pain concentrates in **low-attach / high-warranty-burden** groups (Earnhardt 20%, Koons 70%). Wedge ICP = high documentation burden + low current efficiency.

BUYER / CHAMPION: Corporate **Fixed-Operations Director** (group-level) or **Service Manager** (store-level).
ANTAGONIST / BLOCKER: **DMS incumbents (CDK/Cox/Reynolds)** structurally block integration ("pay to play," won't innovate); + recording-consent/privacy (state dual-party laws; Rilla/Siro precedent).
COMPETITORS: TruVideo (Lantern memo), Plasma (this diligence), XTime/DealerFX/AutoPoint (legacy incumbents), OEM AR-glasses programs (Subaru/Ford).
